INFINITE NEON

A Retrowave Tilemap for MapBox

Ginevra Ryman

Made for a lab project for my Geographic Design in Cartogrphy course at UW Madison, this Mapbox style is called Infinite Neon. The challenge: deconstruct an existing aesthetic style, and create your own tileset in Mapbox Studio.

Retrowave, also known as Synthwave or Outrun, is an electronic music and visual style that began in the early 2000s. It celebrates and romanticises the 1980s and early 1990s, drawing inspiration from movies such as Blade Runner and Tron, and the Outrun video games.

The style blends bright, neon hues such as yellow, magenta, cyan, and green against deep, dark backgrounds. This high-contrast combination creates a futuristic atmosphere that feels both intense and isolating. Glowing, and hazy effects are common, which adds to the digital, otherworldly feeling. Wireframe graphics and 3D grid patterns, reminiscent of early video games, are often included. Fonts draw inspiration from the bold, geometric typography of the 80s and early computer systems, evoking a sense of retro-futurism.

Megacities like Hong Kong are fun to explore, especially with the 3D buildings.

Joshua Tree National Park, and similar natural areas appear dark green, empty, and forbording. Retrowave emphasizes urban areas, and the outlying areas around cities may be empty or dangerous regions held by wastelanders. This is highlighted by the graffiti-like font choice of Outrun future in a bright neon green.

Glowing road networks like these in Los Angeles look like electric cables.

Finally, in the Mediterranean around Greece coastlines glow neon blue, while the water appears as a dark void.
